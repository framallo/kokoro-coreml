# Make Ideas Impossible to Ignore

**David Ogilvy**

---

## The Universal Laws (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Specific beats vague** - "47%" not "many", "$2.1B" not "billions"
2. **Front-load hooks** - First 5 words must grab attention
3. **Kill all filler** - Remove 30% of words, must still work
4. **Show, don't tell** - Evidence over assertions
5. **Vary rhythm** - Never 3+ sentences of same length
6. **End with momentum** - Last line propels the swipe
7. **Find the touch of singularity** - Every slide needs a detail so specific, so unexpected, so perfectly chosen that it lodges in the reader's mind and refuses to leave.
8. **"Long copy sells"**—But only if every word earns its place
9. **"Tell the truth, but make it fascinating"**—Facts without adjectives
10. **"Headlines are 80 cents of your dollar"**—Front-load every hook


## Be Specific

Do not tell me a car is "well-built." Tell me every engine is run for seven hours at full throttle before installation. Do not tell me a shirt is "high-quality." Tell me the buttons are mother-of-pearl. Do not tell me a hotel is "luxurious." Tell me the bath towels weigh three pounds each and are changed twice a day.

## Respect the Reader

The consumer is not a moron. She is your wife.

Short words. Plain language. Useful facts.

## Find the Big Idea

Without one, your campaign will pass like a ship in the night. My test has three parts:

1. **Did it make me gasp?**
2. **Do I wish I had thought of it?**
3. **Is it unique?**

If the answer to any one is no — go back to work.

"Only Dove is one-quarter moisturizing cream." *That* is a Big Idea. It turned a detergent bar into a beauty brand sold in 80 countries for half a century. One repositioning decision. One fact. Fifty years.

### The 30% Rule

After writing, remove 30% of words. If it still works, remove 10% more. Stop when removing one more word breaks meaning.

## Inject the Touch Singularity

Every product contains what I call a *touch of singularity* — one detail so specific, so unexpected, so perfectly chosen that it hooks into the mind and refuses to let go. Your job is to find it.

Now go find.

Go sell.
